Services

Good businesses need more than capital.

What we do.

01

Get your story straight.

You have something worth funding but it's not landing. We help you figure out what you're actually offering and how to say it so the right people say yes.

02

Build the machine, not just run it.

Everything still depends on you. We build the systems for fundraising, BD, and partnerships so the organisation works beyond any single person.

03

Find the right capital.

You know what you need money for but not where to find it — or you know where it is but can't bridge the gap. We know how donors and investors think, and we translate between them.

04

Tell you if this market is real.

You want to invest, expand, or scope a programme in a market you're not close to. We do the groundwork — mapping what's viable, connecting institutional plans with what's actually happening, and structuring the work so it's useful to the people making decisions. Strongest in Sri Lanka and South Asia. Clear-eyed everywhere.

How We Work

Learn. Build. Partner.

Most of our work starts with advisory. Where it goes from there depends on the business and what it actually needs.

Learn

Understand before committing.

We start by listening. What does this business actually do? What's working, what isn't, what does the founder need? We don't show up with a template.

Build

Do the work alongside you.

If the fit is right, we get involved — building systems, sharpening strategy, strengthening operations. Hands-on, not from the sidelines.

Partner

Stay as long as we're useful.

When we believe in what's being built, we back it — with capital, time, or both. The form changes as the business grows. The commitment doesn't.

Investment

When advisory becomes conviction.

Some of our advisory work turns into something deeper. We get to know a business from the inside — its strengths, its gaps, its people. Sometimes we come to believe in it enough to put capital behind it, not just advice.

Serendib Impact Fund I is the vehicle for that. Small tickets, flexible structures — equity, revenue share, convertible notes — whatever fits the venture, not a template. We source through relationships, not databases. And we stay involved long after the cheque clears, because the point was never just the money.
